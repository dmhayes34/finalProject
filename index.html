Blind Board Gaming

Tips and tricks to adapt board games for blind players

Click the images for more information on that game!

About

My name is Dave Hayes. My wife, Sarah, went blind during her early 20s, and was legally blind when we met. She had to give up things she enjoyed like reading books herself (she uses audio books now), and putting together puzzles. We did crossword puzzles together where I would read the clues while we dated. We have since become avid board game players. Some of these games are easy to adapt and some are much harder. I'd like to use this site to illustrate the level of accomodations we needed for games that we enjoy playing.

Pandemic

Concept: Pandemic is a cooperative board game where players work together to stop disease outbreaks and epidemics.
Adaptability: Easy
Tricks: By being cooperative, I could explain to Sarah the cards in our hands, and our position on the board. She has a good sense of geography to know which places would be reasonably close to one another so the map works well.

Troyes

Concept: Troyes is a competitive game set in Medival France. Players are trying to score the most points in a set number of turns, determined by the number of players. Players can earn points by building the cathedral, establishing merchents, having more people in buildings, accumulating gold, and or prestige.
Adaptability: Medium
Tricks: Most of the cards are face up during play, so a lot of information is available to all players and somewhat easily communicated. Sarah tends to get a little overwhelemed with a lot of the options late in the game, and this has caused her to often choose a different game over this one. You do have one secret leader card out of six cards, but with braille labels on only 6 cards this was an easy adjustment to make.

Twighlight Struggle

Concept: Twighlight Struggle is a two player competitive game that has one player take on the role of the United States and the other the Soviet Union during the Cold War 1945-1989.
Adaptability: Excel Required - Advanced
Tricks: This is a card based game. Each player draws up to 8 cards and in later rounds 9 cards from a single deck of cards. I had to create an excel macro that shuffles the deck and deals two hands of cards. Fortunately, the cards are all numbered from 1-103. I have 3 digit code numbers for Sarah's hand and when she enters them into her spreadsheet she can read the card title and text using her screen reader. She also tracks where the influence markers are on the board with her Excel spreadsheet as well.

Trivial Pursuit

Concept: Trivial Pursuit is a triva game. One side of a card has 6 questions on it, and the other side has the 6 answers. The first player to answer a question on each of the pie spaces wins.
Adaptability: Easy
Tricks: Trivial Pursuit is easy to adapt as long as I read all the questions. Some trivia games have the question and answer on the same line, and those trivia games would not work for us.

Civilization: New Dawn

Concept: Civilization: New Dawn is a board game loosely based on the PC game Cvilization VI. You control a leader with some special abiliies and the victory conditions are randomly generated each game.
Adaptability: Medium
Tricks: All of the cards are face up during play, so a lot of information is available to all players and somewhat easily communicated. You have 5 cards in a row and choose which one you want to use that turn it then moves to the bottom of the line (i.e. if you play the card in the 4 spot it moves to the 1 spot, and the others shift up). Creating the board is the only challenge we run into in this game. Both players are supposed to take turns placing tiles, but Sarah can't really participate in that process. I don't like making the board completely on my own. Starting position and what resources are near you is a big part of the game. I'd like to create an excel based map generator (the map tiles are numbered with an a or b side), but I haven't done that yet. Then in cases like this to be fair I ask Sarah "Do you want to start in A or B location?" This creates a random start for both of us.

Star Wars Rebellion

Concept: Star Wars Rebellion is a two player competitive game that has one player take on the role of the Empire and the other the Rebels. The Empire wins by finding and destroying the Rebel Base. The Rebels win by achieving objectives to end the game as fast as possible with their base intact.
Adaptability: Excel Required - Advanced
Tricks: This is a card based game. Each player draws mission cards every turn from their own deck. The Empire has a second deck of cards called Projects, while the Rebels have an Objective deck. I had to create an excel macro that shuffles these decks and deals the appropriate number of cards. I had to make accomodations for "Draw two cards choose one put the other back on the bottom of the deck." The map is more complicated because it's Star Wars planets rather than countries. Sarah has learned the layout of the map over time. The planets are clustered in 8 regions, and we use baseball terms to describe the region locations. There are three regions across the top (Outfield), 4 through the middle (Infield), and one at the middle bottom (Catcher). Sarah being a baseball fan helped with the map.
